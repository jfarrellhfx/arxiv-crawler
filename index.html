Emerging universality classes in thermally-assisted activation of interacting diffusive systems: A perturbative hydrodynamic approach

https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.05839
Vishwajeet Kumar, Arnab Pal, Ohad Shpielberg

Thermal activation of a particle from a deep potential trap follows the Arrhenius law. Recently, this result was generalized for interacting diffusive particles in the trap, revealing two universality classes -- the Arrhenius class and the excluded volume class. The result was demonstrated with the aid of numerical analysis. Here, we present a perturbative hydrodynamic approach to analytically validate the existence and range of validity for the two universality classes.



Vortical currents and reciprocal relations for transport coefficients in the electron hydrodynamic regime

https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.06375
Nisarg Chadha, Subroto Mukerjee

We investigate the hydrodynamic regime in metals with momentum-conserving electron-electron scattering. The conservation of momentum results in well-defined dynamics whose effects we investigate via the relevant continuity equations. Studying semiclassical thermoelectric transport in the presence of a Berry curvature, we find anomalous contributions to the charge and heat transport currents arising from gradients of the velocity field. These contributions are non-vanishing for systems lacking centro-symmetry, and the corresponding transport coefficients do not obey the standard Onsager reciprocity relations. Instead, we show that the response coefficients relating the currents to the stress tensor obey independent reciprocity relations with the stress tensor and thus exhibit cross-tensor effects of the charge density and the entropy density with the momentum density. The requirement of the lack of centrosymmetry is shown to arise as a consequence of the Curie symmetry principle, which prevents cross-tensor effects in isotropic systems.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.05294

Here, by building a library of 590 surfaces and grain boundaries of 11 relevant solid electrolytes, including halides, oxides, and sulfides, their electronic, mechanical, and thermodynamic characteristics are linked to the functional properties of polycrystalline solid electrolytes. In certain non-Hermitian topological systems, conventional BBC becomes invalid in the presence of the non-Hermitian skin effect (NHSE), which manifests as distinct energy spectra under the periodic and open boundary conditions and massive eigenstate localization at boundaries. In this work, we introduce a scheme to induce NHSE without breaking conventional BBC, dubbed as the topologically compatible NHSE (TC-NHSE). Momentum-dependent quasiparticle properties of the Fermi polaron from the functional renormalization group

https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.05318

We study theoretically the lifetimes of attractive and repulsive Fermi polarons, as well as the molecule at finite momentum in three dimensions. To this end, we develop a new technique that allows for the computation of Green's functions in the whole complex frequency plane using exact analytical continuation within the functional renormalization group. The improved numerical stability and reduced computational cost of this method yield access to previously inaccessible momentum-dependent quasiparticle properties of low-lying excited states. While conventional approaches like the non-selfconsistent $T$-matrix approximation method cannot determine these lifetimes, we are able to find the momentum-dependent lifetime at different interaction strengths of both the attractive and repulsive polaron as well as the molecule.
